CNC MACHINING

Computer Numerical Control (CNC) machining is a traditional manufacturing process often referred to as “subtractive” due to its nature of cutting away unwanted material from a solid block of raw stock or from pre-existing parts with a sharp tool to achieve a desired geometry. CNC machining encompasses milling, turning and grinding delivering high-accuracy with very tight tolerance.

 The most common materials used for CNC machining:

  • ABS
  • Polycarbonate
  • LDPE
  • Delrin
  • Glass-Filled Nylon
  • Nylon
  • Polypro
  • Peek
  • Aluminum
  • Stainless Steel
  • Titanium
  • Zinc

Fusion Prototype Manufacturing experienced CNC machinists take great care in all machining projects and oversee the manufacturing of low and high quantities of metal and plastic parts whether it is: Cosmetic Models, Precision Sub-components or Specialty Patterns and Tooling.
